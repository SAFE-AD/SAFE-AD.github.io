SAFE-AD: Adaptive and Forward-Thinking Autonomous Driving Empowered by Long Chain-of-Thought Reasoning

Abstract

The integration of large language models (LLMs) into autonomous driving systems has introduced new opportunities but faces challenges such as limited reasoning capabilities and adaptability in complex scenarios. Traditional single-path reasoning way risks constraining the model's reasoning flexibility. To overcome these limitations, we propose SAFE-AD (Systematic, Adaptive, Forward-Thinking, and Efficient Autonomous Driving), a novel framework that incorporates long chain-of-thought reasoning to enhance decision-making and address the challenges of long-tail scenarios. SAFE-AD features four key components: an Environmental Encoder that captures and processes critical environmental information, a Tree Construction Model that systematically explores potential actions, a Process Reward Model designed to evaluate and prioritize decisions based on human-like driving preferences, and a Memory Module that enables the system to learn from past experiences and adapt to new environments. Experimental results in diverse and complex driving scenarios demonstrate that SAFE-AD significantly outperforms existing knowledge-driven and reinforcement learning-based methods, achieving superior driving performance.

Overview

We introduce SAFE-AD (Systematic, Adaptive, Forward Thinking, and Efficient Autonomous Driving), a framework designed to build long chains of thought to address complex challenges in autonomous driving. As shown in Framework, SAFE-AD comprises four key components: the environmental encoder, the tree construction model, the process reward model and the memory module. When the vehicle encounters a new environment, the environmental encoder first captures and processes environmental information, generating high-level descriptions of critical features. These descriptions are then inputted into the tree construction model, which incrementally builds a decision tree where each node represents an action with an initially neutral (zero) value. Subsequently, the process reward model analyzes the tree structure, assigning a reward to each node based on future states estimation. Using these rewards, SAFE-AD identifies the optimal policy and selects the most suitable action for the given scenario. The assignment of rewards to each node in the decision tree is a challenging task. To address this, SAFE-AD employs a memory bank that records the historical states of the ego vehicle and valid past experiences. These historical states are utilized by the process reward model to infer future states, which are critical for reasonable reward estimation. The whole process is iterative, with the tree construction model and the process reward model working in tandem to facilitate long chain-of-thought reasoning. This iterative reasoning process enhances the system's ability to make more intelligent and informed decisions.
